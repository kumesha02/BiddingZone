<p>A&nbsp;<strong>blender</strong>&nbsp;(sometimes called a&nbsp;<strong>mixer</strong>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<strong>liquidiser</strong>&nbsp;in&nbsp;<a title="British English" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_English">British English</a>) is a&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Kitchen appliance" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kitchen_appliance">kitchen</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Laboratory" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laboratory">laboratory</a>&nbsp;appliance used to mix, crush,&nbsp;<a title="Pur&eacute;e" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pur%C3%A9e">pur&eacute;e</a>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<a title="Emulsion" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emulsion">emulsify</a>&nbsp;food and other substances. A stationary blender consists of a blender container with a rotating metal blade at the bottom, powered by an electric motor that is in the base. Some powerful models can also crush ice and other frozen foods. The newer&nbsp;<a title="Immersion blender" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immersion_blender">immersion blender</a>&nbsp;configuration has a motor on top connected by a shaft to a rotating blade at the bottom, which can be used with any container.</p>
