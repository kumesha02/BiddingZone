<p><strong>Raspberry Pi</strong>&nbsp;(<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a title="Help:IPA/English" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English">/<span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="/aɪ/: 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span>/</a></span></span>) is a series of small&nbsp;<a title="Single-board computer" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single-board_computer">single-board computers</a>&nbsp;(SBCs) developed in the&nbsp;<a title="United Kingdom" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>&nbsp;by the&nbsp;<a title="Raspberry Pi Foundation" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi_Foundation">Raspberry Pi Foundation</a>&nbsp;in association with&nbsp;<a title="Broadcom Inc." href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadcom_Inc.">Broadcom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>&nbsp;The Raspberry Pi project originally leaned towards the promotion of teaching basic&nbsp;<a title="Computer science" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_science">computer science</a>&nbsp;in schools.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>&nbsp;The original model became more popular than anticipated,<sup id="cite_ref-1000x_18-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-1000x-18">[18]</a></sup>&nbsp;selling outside its&nbsp;<a title="Target market" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Target_market">target market</a>&nbsp;for uses such as&nbsp;<a title="Robotics" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robotics">robotics</a>. It is widely used in many areas, such as for&nbsp;<a title="Automatic weather station" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automatic_weather_station">weather monitoring</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>&nbsp;because of its low cost, modularity, and open design. It is typically used by computer and electronic hobbyists, due to its adoption of the&nbsp;<a title="HDMI" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HDMI">HDMI</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="USB" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USB">USB</a>&nbsp;standards.</p>
<p>After the release of the second board type, the Raspberry Pi Foundation set up a new entity, named Raspberry Pi Trading, and installed&nbsp;<a title="Eben Upton" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eben_Upton">Eben Upton</a>&nbsp;as&nbsp;<a title="Chief executive officer" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_executive_officer">CEO</a>, with the responsibility of developing technology.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>&nbsp;The Foundation was rededicated as an educational charity for promoting the teaching of basic computer science in schools and developing countries. Most Pis are made in a&nbsp;<a title="Sony" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sony">Sony</a>&nbsp;factory in&nbsp;<a title="Pencoed" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pencoed">Pencoed</a>, Wales,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>&nbsp;while others are made in China and Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raspberry_Pi#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
