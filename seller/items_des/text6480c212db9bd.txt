<p>A&nbsp;<strong>jump wire</strong>&nbsp;(also known as&nbsp;<strong>jumper</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>jumper wire</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>DuPont wire</strong>) is an&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Electrical wire" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_wire">electrical wire</a>, or group of them in a cable, with a connector or pin at each end (or sometimes without them&nbsp;&ndash; simply "tinned"), which is normally used to interconnect the components of a&nbsp;<a title="Breadboard" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breadboard">breadboard</a>&nbsp;or other prototype or test circuit, internally or with other equipment or components, without soldering.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jump_wire#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Individual jump wires are fitted by inserting their "end connectors" into the slots provided in a breadboard, the&nbsp;<a title="Pin header" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pin_header">header connector</a>&nbsp;of a circuit board, or a piece of test equipment.</p>
